# Modeling Principles

## Pathways are sequences

An adaptive pathway is a sequence of actions, reviews, triggers, and possible switches over time.

## Triggers make adaptation operational

Adaptation without triggers is only a promise. Trigger points connect evidence to action.

## Monitoring must be actionable

Indicators should have owners, thresholds, review cadence, and escalation authority.

## Option value matters

A pathway can be valuable because it preserves future alternatives, reduces lock-in, or lowers switching costs.

## Governance enables adaptation

Adaptive pathways require decision rights, stakeholder review, funding flexibility, and durable decision records.
