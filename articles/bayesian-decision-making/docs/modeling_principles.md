# Modeling Principles

## Belief updating is not action selection

Bayesian updating produces posterior beliefs. Decision-making begins when those posterior beliefs are connected to actions, utilities, losses, thresholds, or governance rules.

## Priors should be explicit

Priors may come from data, expert judgment, institutional memory, or reference classes. They should be documented and sensitivity-tested.

## Evidence must be diagnostic

Evidence updates beliefs strongly only when it is more likely under one hypothesis than another.

## Sequential learning requires review triggers

If evidence arrives over time, decision-makers should define posterior thresholds that trigger action, escalation, monitoring, or revision.
