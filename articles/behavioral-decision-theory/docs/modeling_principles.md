# Modeling Principles

## Formal benchmarks still matter

Expected value, expected utility, decision trees, MCDA, and Bayesian models provide useful analytical baselines.

## Behavioral diagnostics reveal human interpretation risk

Reference points, loss aversion, framing, anchoring, availability, salience, overconfidence, and probability weighting can change judgment.

## Behavioral patterns are process risks

Bias is not merely an individual flaw. It is often a decision-environment and governance issue.

## Choice architecture is never neutral

Defaults, labels, order, categories, visual emphasis, and comparison sets shape behavior.

## Records support accountability

Decision records preserve frames, assumptions, reference points, probabilities, values, dissent, rationale, and review triggers before hindsight rewrites them.
