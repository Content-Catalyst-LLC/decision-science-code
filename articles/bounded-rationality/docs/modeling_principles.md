# Modeling Principles

## Bounded rationality is reason under constraint

Decision-makers are not omniscient optimizers. They search, simplify, satisfice, use routines, and learn over time.

## Alternatives are not automatically complete

Many decisions require alternative discovery, option generation, and search design.

## Satisficing depends on aspiration levels

"Good enough" should be explicit, justified, and revisable.

## Search has cost

More analysis is useful only when it is likely to change the decision enough to justify delay and cost.

## Records support learning

Decision records preserve search limits, assumptions, thresholds, alternatives, stopping rules, and outcomes.
