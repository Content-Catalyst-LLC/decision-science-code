# Modeling Principles

## Risk propagates through structure

Cascading risk depends on dependency links, central nodes, thresholds, buffers, common-mode exposure, and response capacity.

## Local optimization can create systemic fragility

Each actor may make locally reasonable choices that reduce slack, concentrate exposure, or shift risk elsewhere.

## Common-mode failure defeats apparent diversification

Multiple suppliers, models, agencies, systems, or decision teams are not truly independent if they share the same hidden failure mode.

## Buffers and containment are decision variables

Redundancy, slack, modularity, isolation points, fallback pathways, and graceful degradation shape whether failure spreads.

## Governance must cross boundaries

Systemic risks cross organizational, technical, legal, jurisdictional, and sectoral boundaries. Accountability must be designed accordingly.
