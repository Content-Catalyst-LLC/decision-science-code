# Systemic Decision Failure

Systemic decision failure occurs when a decision process produces, amplifies, ignores, or fails to contain risk across a system.

Common sources:
- narrow boundaries
- local optimization
- fragmented authority
- delayed feedback
- shared assumptions
- unaccountable handoffs
- poor monitoring
- weak escalation rights

Systemic decision failure occurs when the decision process is smaller than the consequences of the decision.
