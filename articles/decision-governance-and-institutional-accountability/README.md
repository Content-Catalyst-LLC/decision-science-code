# Decision Governance and Institutional Accountability

This companion directory supports the article **"Decision Governance and Institutional Accountability."**

The workflows operationalize decision governance as a reproducible decision-science problem involving decision rights, authority, evidence standards, accountability chains, review processes, decision records, internal control, monitoring, corrective capacity, responsibility gaps, and governance learning.

## Core Claim

Decision governance turns decision science into institutional practice. A strong decision system does not merely produce a choice. It defines decision rights, evidence standards, review processes, documentation, challenge mechanisms, escalation thresholds, implementation responsibility, monitoring obligations, and learning loops.

## Modeling Principles

1. Treat governance as the institutional architecture of decision-making.
2. Define decision rights for recommendation, review, approval, implementation, monitoring, revision, escalation, and stop authority.
3. Match evidence standards to decision stakes, uncertainty, reversibility, and public impact.
4. Preserve decision records that show context, authority, evidence, assumptions, dissent, rationale, and review triggers.
5. Identify responsibility gaps where influence exceeds accountability.
6. Build independent challenge into high-stakes decisions.
7. Connect approval to implementation ownership and monitoring.
8. Use internal controls and assurance to test whether governance works in practice.
9. Track accountability drift over time.
10. Turn outcomes, audits, complaints, incidents, and after-action review into governance revision.

Rscript r/run_all_decision_governance_workflows.R
