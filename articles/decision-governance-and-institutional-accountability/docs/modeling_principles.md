# Modeling Principles

## Decision governance is institutional architecture

Governance defines how decisions move from problem recognition to action, monitoring, correction, and learning.

## Accountability requires traceability

Decision authority, evidence, rationale, implementation, monitoring, and corrective capacity should be traceable.

## Responsibility gaps matter

A responsibility gap appears when an actor has decision influence without matching accountability.

## Governance should be risk-tiered

High-stakes decisions require stronger evidence, review, records, controls, and monitoring than low-risk decisions.

## Learning closes the accountability loop

Governance improves when outcomes, incidents, audits, complaints, and review findings change future decision practice.
