# Modeling Principles

## Bias reduction is process design

Awareness alone is weak. Decision hygiene changes the conditions under which judgment occurs.

## Bias and noise are different

Bias is systematic directional error. Noise is unwanted variability. Decision hygiene should reduce both where possible.

## Evidence must be structured

Claims, sources, evidence quality, base rates, assumptions, missing evidence, and disconfirming evidence should be made explicit.

## Dissent should be designed

Dissent is not disruption. It is a decision-quality resource when structured and documented.

## Learning requires records

Decision records preserve assumptions, uncertainty, confidence, dissent, selected actions, and review triggers before outcomes are known.
