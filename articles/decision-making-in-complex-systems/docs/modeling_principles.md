# Modeling Principles

## Decisions change the system

A decision is not only an input. It becomes part of the system's future structure.

## Feedback changes outcomes over time

Reinforcing and balancing loops can amplify, neutralize, or reverse an intervention.

## Delays distort interpretation

Early outcomes may not reveal long-term system behavior.

## Emergence requires system-level indicators

Local performance can improve while system-level resilience, legitimacy, or stability declines.

## Adaptation changes the decision environment

People, organizations, markets, institutions, technologies, and adversaries may respond to the intervention.

## Decision records preserve accountability

Complexity does not remove accountability. It changes what responsible accountability must document.
