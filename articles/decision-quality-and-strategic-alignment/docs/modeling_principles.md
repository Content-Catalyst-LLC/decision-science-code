# Modeling Principles

## Decision quality is not outcome quality

Good outcomes can follow weak processes and bad outcomes can follow strong processes.

## Strategic alignment is operational

Alignment should be visible in criteria, trade-offs, resource allocation, implementation, and decision records.

## Strategy becomes real through decisions

Strategy is enacted by repeated choices, not merely written statements.

## Drift is a pattern-level signal

One decision may look aligned in isolation while repeated decisions reveal strategic fragmentation.

## Learning requires records

Decision records preserve assumptions, alternatives, trade-offs, uncertainty, dissent, strategic fit, and review triggers before outcomes are known.
