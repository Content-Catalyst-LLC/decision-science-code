# Modeling Principles

## Process quality is not outcome quality

Outcomes are shaped by the action, the state of the world, implementation conditions, and random disturbances. Decision quality evaluates the process before outcomes are known.

## Architecture matters

Decision quality includes framing, alternatives, evidence, uncertainty, values, safeguards, systems reasoning, accountability, and learning design.

## Luck must be separated from skill

A favorable outcome from a weak process should not be mistaken for decision quality. An unfavorable outcome from a strong process should not automatically be treated as decision failure.

## Decision records are essential

Decision records preserve what was known, assumed, valued, contested, and expected at the time of commitment.
