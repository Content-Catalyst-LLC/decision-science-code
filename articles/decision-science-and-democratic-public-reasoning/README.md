# Decision Science and Democratic Public Reasoning

This companion directory supports the article **"Decision Science and Democratic Public Reasoning."**

The workflows operationalize democratic public reasoning as a reproducible decision-science problem involving public value, evidence transparency, participation quality, procedural fairness, contestability, equity review, accountability, uncertainty communication, public trust, harm signals, review triggers, and public decision records.

## Core Claim

Decision science serves democracy best when it strengthens public reasoning rather than replacing it. In democratic contexts, good decision-making is not only about choosing the option with the highest expected value. It is about making reasons visible, evidence contestable, trade-offs understandable, authority accountable, uncertainty honest, and affected people meaningfully included.

## Modeling Principles

1. Treat public decisions as exercises of collective authority, not merely technical optimization.
2. Make evidence, assumptions, criteria, uncertainty, and value trade-offs visible.
3. Distinguish technical evidence from democratic value judgment.
4. Design participation around real decision points, not after decisions are effectively settled.
5. Evaluate public legitimacy through transparency, fairness, participation, contestability, and accountability.
6. Preserve dissent, public input, excluded alternatives, and uncertainty in decision records.
7. Use models and metrics as public reasoning tools, not shields against public accountability.
8. Communicate uncertainty honestly enough for decisions to be revised without appearing arbitrary.
9. Track public trust, legitimacy, harm, equity, contestability, and responsiveness after implementation.
10. Turn outcomes, complaints, audits, public feedback, and review triggers into institutional learning.

## Quick Start

From this article directory:

```bash
python3 python/run_all_democratic_reasoning_workflows.py
Rscript r/run_all_democratic_reasoning_workflows.R
```

Outputs are written to:

```text
outputs/tables/
outputs/figures/
outputs/decision_records/
```
