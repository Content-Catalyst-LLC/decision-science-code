# Article Notes: Decision Science and Democratic Public Reasoning

This article examines how structured decision methods can support democratic legitimacy, public deliberation, civic trust, transparent trade-offs, accountable evidence use, and collective judgment without reducing public decisions to expert optimization or technocratic control.

Core themes:
- democratic public reasoning
- evidence and legitimacy
- public values and trade-offs
- participation and deliberation
- stakeholder standing
- expertise without technocracy
- uncertainty communication
- model governance
- contestability
- public trust
- public decision records
- institutional learning
