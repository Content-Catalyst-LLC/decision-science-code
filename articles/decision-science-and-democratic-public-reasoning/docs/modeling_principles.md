# Modeling Principles

## Decision science should support public reasoning

Structured methods clarify public choices when they make evidence, values, assumptions, uncertainty, and trade-offs visible.

## Democratic legitimacy is not technical performance

A decision may be analytically strong yet democratically weak if participation, transparency, equity, contestability, or accountability is poor.

## Public values should be explicit

Criteria, weights, thresholds, rights, constraints, and trade-offs should be documented and open to public challenge.

## Expertise should not become technocracy

Experts can clarify evidence and consequences, but democratic institutions remain responsible for public judgment.

## Accountability requires learning

Public decisions should be monitored, reviewed, corrected, and revised when outcomes or public evidence require it.
