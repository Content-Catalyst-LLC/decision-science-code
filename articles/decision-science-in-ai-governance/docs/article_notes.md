# Article Notes: Decision Science in AI Governance

This article examines how organizations, governments, public institutions, developers, deployers, regulators, and affected communities make structured decisions about artificial intelligence under uncertainty, risk, accountability, human rights, institutional constraint, and rapid technological change.

Core themes:
- AI governance as a decision system
- AI lifecycle decision points
- risk classification
- evidence thresholds
- model risk and sociotechnical risk
- human oversight
- fairness and distributional impact
- transparency and contestability
- data governance
- generative AI risk
- procurement and vendor risk
- monitoring and drift
- incident response
- decision records
