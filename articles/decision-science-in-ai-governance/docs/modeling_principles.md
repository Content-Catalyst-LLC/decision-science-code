# Modeling Principles

## AI governance is a decision system

AI governance determines what systems are approved, constrained, monitored, challenged, revised, or stopped.

## Context defines risk

The same model capability can be low-risk in one workflow and unacceptable in another. Use context, affected population, stakes, autonomy, reversibility, and institutional capacity matter.

## Evidence thresholds should match stakes

Higher-stakes systems require stronger validation, documentation, oversight, subgroup analysis, security review, and contestability.

## Oversight must be meaningful

Human oversight requires authority, information, time, expertise, independence, and accountability.

## Governance must be adaptive

Deployment approval is conditional. Drift, incidents, misuse, complaints, equity problems, and vendor updates should trigger review.
