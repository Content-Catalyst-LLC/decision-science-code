# Modeling Principles

## Risk is embedded in decisions

Financial risk becomes meaningful through choices about exposures, limits, capital, liquidity, hedging, model trust, and escalation.

## Models are useful but incomplete

A model structures attention, assumptions, and decision authority. It should support judgment rather than close judgment.

## Stress matters more than averages

Financial institutions fail in the tails, during liquidity stress, under correlation breakdown, and when governance cannot act quickly enough.

## Incentives shape interpretation

Risk appetite, compensation, business-line pressure, and challenge culture determine how risk information is used.

## Accountability requires records

Decision records preserve assumptions, overlays, stress results, dissent, alternatives, approval authority, and revision triggers.
