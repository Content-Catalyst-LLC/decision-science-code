# Modeling Principles

## Healthcare decisions are high stakes

Healthcare decisions affect diagnosis, treatment, safety, suffering, survival, access, equity, cost, institutional capacity, and public trust.

## Evidence informs but does not decide everything

Evidence must be integrated with clinical judgment, patient values, uncertainty, resource constraints, and ethical responsibility.

## Diagnosis is probabilistic

Clinical reasoning updates beliefs as new evidence arrives. Test results should be interpreted in relation to pre-test probability, sensitivity, specificity, and consequences of error.

## Systems shape outcomes

Care quality depends on workflows, queues, staffing, capacity, handoffs, communication, technology, incentives, and safety culture.

## Accountability requires records

Decision records preserve evidence, assumptions, patient preferences, alternatives, safety concerns, equity issues, and revision triggers.
