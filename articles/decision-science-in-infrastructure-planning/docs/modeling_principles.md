# Modeling Principles

## Infrastructure is a long-lived decision

Infrastructure assets create public obligations across decades. Decision quality must account for future uncertainty, maintenance, service continuity, and intergenerational effects.

## Service matters more than project identity

The decision should begin with the public service need: access, safety, reliability, resilience, affordability, environmental quality, or health.

## Uncertainty requires robustness

Demand, climate, technology, funding, land use, and institutional capacity can change. Robustness and adaptive pathways help decisions remain defensible across futures.

## Equity is part of decision quality

Infrastructure distributes benefits, burdens, risks, access, affordability, and exposure. Distribution must be explicit.

## Accountability requires records

Decision records preserve alternatives, assumptions, scenarios, trade-offs, equity concerns, selected action, and review triggers.
