# Behavioral Insights

Behavioral decision theory matters in policy because people do not always respond to information, incentives, risks, or rules as formal models predict.

Relevant concepts:
- defaults
- framing
- loss aversion
- present bias
- administrative burden
- trust
- identity
- social norms
- cognitive load

Behavioral policy should be transparent, accountable, and not used as a substitute for structural reform where structural barriers are the real problem.
