# Equity and Legitimacy

Equity is part of the policy decision problem because policy determines who is protected, who is burdened, who benefits, and who has voice.

Legitimacy questions:
- Whose risk is reduced?
- Whose burden increases?
- Who defines success?
- Who can contest the policy?
- Who is excluded from evidence and deliberation?
- Which values cannot be traded away?
- How are future stakeholders represented?

Public trust is often damaged less by the existence of trade-offs than by the concealment of them.
