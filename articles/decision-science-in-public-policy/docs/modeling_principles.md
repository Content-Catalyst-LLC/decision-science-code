# Modeling Principles

## Public policy is multi-objective

Policy decisions must often balance efficiency, equity, resilience, feasibility, legitimacy, sustainability, and institutional capacity.

## Evidence informs but does not decide

Evidence supports judgment, but values, trade-offs, risk tolerance, and public legitimacy remain part of the decision problem.

## Policy acts through institutions

Implementation capacity, decision rights, incentives, staffing, legal authority, data systems, and institutional trust shape outcomes.

## Systems respond

Citizens, firms, agencies, courts, markets, and political actors respond to policy in ways that can amplify, weaken, redirect, or reverse intended effects.

## Accountability requires memory

Decision records preserve evidence, assumptions, alternatives, trade-offs, dissent, stakeholder concerns, and revision triggers.
