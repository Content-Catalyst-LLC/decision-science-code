# Decision Science in Sustainability

This companion directory supports the article **"Decision Science in Sustainability."**

The workflows operationalize sustainability decision science as a reproducible decision-science problem involving sustainability strategy comparison, multi-objective analysis, scenario robustness, ecological thresholds, resource pressure, adaptive policy response, environmental justice, implementation capacity, public trust, governance delay, and decision-record scaffolding.

## Core Claim

Sustainability decision-making is not simply about choosing greener options inside a fixed world. It is about making judgments inside systems whose boundaries, feedback loops, risks, trade-offs, and value conflicts are themselves contested. Decision science helps make those judgments more explicit, monitorable, and revisable over time.

## Modeling Principles

1. Treat sustainability decisions as multi-objective, long-horizon, and distributional.
2. Make environmental, social, economic, governance, resilience, and justice objectives explicit.
3. Distinguish ordinary trade-offs from ecological thresholds and safe minimum standards.
4. Evaluate strategies across plausible futures rather than one forecast.
5. Track resource stocks, extraction, regeneration, pressure, and adaptive response.
6. Include equity, environmental justice, public trust, and intergenerational responsibility.
7. Connect strategy to implementation capacity, incentives, governance, and monitoring.
8. Use robust decision-making and adaptive pathways under deep uncertainty.
9. Preserve assumptions, thresholds, evidence, uncertainty, trade-offs, and dissent.
10. Use decision records to support accountability across time.

## Quick Start

From this article directory:

```bash
python3 python/run_all_sustainability_workflows.py
Rscript r/run_all_sustainability_workflows.R
```

Outputs are written to:

```text
outputs/tables/
outputs/figures/
outputs/decision_records/
```
