# Decision Modeling Principles

## 1. Frame the decision
Clarify the actual decision before scoring alternatives.

## 2. Generate alternatives
Poor decisions often begin with an impoverished option set.

## 3. Represent uncertainty
Use probabilities where appropriate, scenarios where probabilities are weak, and robustness where uncertainty is deep.

## 4. Surface values
Criteria and weights are value judgments. They should be explicit and contestable.

## 5. Test assumptions
Sensitivity analysis shows whether conclusions are stable or fragile.

## 6. Distinguish models from legitimacy
A model can clarify reasoning, but it cannot by itself make a decision legitimate.

## 7. Preserve learning
Decision records should support post-decision review and institutional learning.
