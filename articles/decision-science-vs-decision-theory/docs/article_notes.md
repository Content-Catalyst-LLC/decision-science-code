# Article Notes: Decision Science vs. Decision Theory

Decision theory provides the formal foundations of rational choice. Decision science extends those foundations into real-world conditions where cognition is bounded, evidence is incomplete, values are plural, institutions shape action, and uncertainty may be deep.

Core themes:
- decision theory as formal rational choice
- decision science as applied structured judgment
- normative, descriptive, and prescriptive decision work
- expected utility and Bayesian updating
- bounded rationality and satisficing
- institutional context and implementation constraints
- regret, robustness, and adaptive decision-making
- decision records and accountability
