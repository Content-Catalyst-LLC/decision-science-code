# Ethics of Decision Science

This companion directory supports the article **"Ethics of Decision Science."**

The workflows operationalize ethical decision science as a reproducible problem involving values, criteria, stakeholder standing, distributional effects, transparency, contestability, uncertainty, false precision, power, accountability, and decision-record scaffolding.

## Core Claim

Ethical decision science requires more than better tools. It requires explicit attention to values, distribution, legitimacy, consent, transparency, contestability, power, uncertainty, institutional responsibility, and the lived consequences of decisions.

## Modeling Principles

1. Treat ethics as part of the decision system, not as a final compliance step.
2. Make values, criteria, weights, constraints, and assumptions explicit.
3. Distinguish aggregate value from distributional justice.
4. Evaluate stakeholder standing, voice, participation, and contestability.
5. Treat some ethical requirements as constraints rather than tradeable criteria.
6. Avoid false precision when evidence is weak or values are contested.
7. Examine power, institutional incentives, conflicts of interest, and decision authority.
8. Connect transparency to explanation, challenge, correction, and remedy.
9. Preserve dissent, uncertainty, trade-offs, and review triggers in decision records.
10. Treat decision methods as supports for accountable judgment, not replacements for it.

## Quick Start
