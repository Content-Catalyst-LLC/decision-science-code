# Modeling Principles

## The system responds

A policy is not simply implemented into a passive environment. It changes incentives, flows, perceptions, and behavior.

## Feedback can amplify or neutralize

Reinforcing feedback can accelerate change. Balancing feedback can stabilize or resist change.

## Delays distort interpretation

Early outcomes may conceal later consequences. Lagged effects can cause overcorrection or premature abandonment.

## Stocks matter

Backlog, debt, emissions, trust, fatigue, and capacity are accumulations. They respond differently from one-time events.

## Policy resistance is structural

Resistance often reflects system structure rather than simple opposition or poor execution.

## Decision records support learning

Feedback assumptions, delay estimates, resistance risks, monitoring indicators, and review triggers should be documented before implementation.
