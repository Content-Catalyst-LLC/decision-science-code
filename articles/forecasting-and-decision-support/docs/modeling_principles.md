# Modeling Principles

## Forecasts are not decisions

A forecast estimates what may happen. A decision determines what should be done.

## Forecasts should be decision-relevant

A forecast has practical value when it affects action, timing, monitoring, mitigation, or review.

## Uncertainty should be explicit

Point forecasts should be paired with uncertainty ranges, probabilities, intervals, scenarios, or sensitivity analysis.

## Thresholds connect forecasts to action

Forecast-triggered decisions require explicit thresholds that account for consequences and costs of error.

## Forecasts should be scored and reviewed

Forecast quality should be evaluated through error, calibration, horizon effects, and decision value.
