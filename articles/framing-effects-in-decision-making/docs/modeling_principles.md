# Modeling Principles

## Frames are part of decision architecture

Every decision is presented through some structure, language, comparison set, interface, or baseline.

## Equivalent descriptions should be tested

Decision quality improves when gain and loss frames, absolute and relative risks, and action and inaction frames are compared.

## Reference points must be explicit

The same outcome can appear as success, failure, gain, loss, progress, or regression depending on the baseline.

## Framing is an ethical issue

Frames influence judgment and can clarify, manipulate, obscure, or exclude.

## Decision records should preserve frames

Future review requires knowing how the decision was presented when judgment occurred.
