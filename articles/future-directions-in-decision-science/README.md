# Future Directions in Decision Science

This companion directory supports the article **"Future Directions in Decision Science."**

The workflows operationalize future decision science as a reproducible decision-science problem involving AI-assisted decision support, governance maturity, uncertainty capability, participatory legitimacy, reproducibility, systems modeling, ethical accountability, adaptive capacity, institutional learning, failure risk, review triggers, and decision records.

## Core Claim

Future decision science must move beyond narrow optimization. It must integrate human judgment, artificial intelligence, uncertainty analysis, ethical reasoning, public legitimacy, systems thinking, adaptive governance, reproducible workflows, participatory methods, and institutional accountability.

## Modeling Principles

1. Treat decisions as systems, not isolated choices.
2. Use AI as accountable decision support, not as a substitute for judgment.
3. Build governance before scaling advanced decision tools.
4. Design for deep uncertainty with robust, adaptive, and scenario-based methods.
5. Connect decision science to democratic legitimacy, participation, and contestability.
6. Represent complex systems, feedback, path dependence, cascading risk, and resilience.
7. Make workflows reproducible through data, code, assumptions, outputs, and records.
8. Integrate ethics, equity, power, and remedy into decision architecture.
9. Communicate uncertainty honestly and define review triggers.
10. Use monitoring, audits, complaints, incidents, and new evidence to improve future decision capacity.

## Quick Start

From this article directory:

```bash
python3 python/run_all_future_decision_science_workflows.py
Rscript r/run_all_future_decision_science_workflows.R
```

Outputs are written to:

```text
outputs/tables/
outputs/figures/
outputs/decision_records/
```
