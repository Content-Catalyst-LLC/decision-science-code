# Modeling Principles

## Independence matters

Groups benefit from distributed judgment only when initial estimates are preserved before social influence begins.

## Influence is not neutral

Authority, status, expertise, confidence, and hierarchy can determine whose judgment shapes the group.

## Unique information must be elicited

Shared information often dominates discussion even when unshared information is decision-critical.

## Dissent is evidence

Dissent may reveal hidden assumptions, weak evidence, value conflict, implementation risk, or stakeholder harm.

## Records support learning

Decision records preserve evidence, confidence, dissent, decision rules, and review triggers before hindsight rewrites the group decision.
