# Modeling Principles

## Historical paradigms are decision lenses

Expected value, expected utility, subjective probability, satisficing, regret, and robustness answer different questions.

## Formal rigor must be matched to decision context

A mathematically coherent model can still fail if probabilities, utilities, outcomes, or institutional constraints are poorly specified.

## Behavioral realism matters

Bounded rationality and behavioral bias explain why idealized optimization is not enough for applied decision science.

## Systems context matters

Dynamic systems, feedback, delay, and adaptation change what counts as a good decision.

## Decision records matter

Historical analysis should preserve assumptions, model choices, probabilities, values, and criteria for later review.
