# Modeling Principles

## Judgment has structure

A judgment should distinguish prior belief, evidence, posterior belief, forecast probability, confidence, and action threshold.

## Evidence must be evaluated

Evidence can be reliable, noisy, biased, missing, ambiguous, or weakly diagnostic.

## Confidence must be calibrated

A confident judgment is not necessarily an accurate judgment. Confidence should be scored against outcomes over repeated cases.

## Bias is process risk

Anchoring, availability, base-rate neglect, confirmation bias, overconfidence, and framing effects can distort judgment unless the process is designed to detect them.

## Records support learning

Decision records preserve beliefs, assumptions, confidence, evidence, and thresholds before outcomes are known.
