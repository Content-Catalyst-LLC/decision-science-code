# Uncertainty

Uncertainty refers to conditions where outcomes, probabilities, evidence, models, or values are incomplete, unstable, ambiguous, or contested.

Useful distinctions:
- risk: probabilities are known or estimable
- uncertainty: probabilities are unclear or unstable
- ambiguity: evidence supports multiple interpretations
- ignorance: important unknowns are not recognized
- deep uncertainty: probabilities, models, outcomes, or values are contested
