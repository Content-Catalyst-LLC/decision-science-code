# Modeling Principles

## MCDA is structured judgment

MCDA does not remove judgment. It makes judgment visible enough to examine, challenge, and improve.

## Criteria define what the decision can see

Missing, duplicated, vague, or poorly measured criteria can distort the entire analysis.

## Weights encode values

Weights reflect priorities. They should be documented, tested, and interpreted as value judgments.

## Rankings are conditional

Rankings depend on criteria, scores, weights, normalization, thresholds, and aggregation logic.

## Sensitivity is mandatory

A serious MCDA process tests whether rankings are stable under plausible changes in assumptions.

## Records support accountability

Decision records preserve alternatives, criteria, scores, weights, sensitivity results, dissent, and rationale.
