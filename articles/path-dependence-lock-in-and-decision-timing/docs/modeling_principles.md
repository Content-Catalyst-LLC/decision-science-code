# Modeling Principles

## History matters

A decision is not made on a blank slate. Past investments, routines, standards, contracts, capabilities, and expectations shape the present choice set.

## Lock-in accumulates

Technical, economic, institutional, behavioral, political, and infrastructure dependencies can make later change costly.

## Timing is a decision variable

Acting, waiting, piloting, staging, reversing, or preserving multiple paths changes future flexibility.

## Sunk costs are not switching costs

Sunk costs are unrecoverable past investments. Switching costs are future transition burdens. They should not be confused.

## Option value can decay

Delay can preserve information, but delay can also narrow future options as the current path hardens.

## Decision records preserve accountability

Path-dependent decisions require durable records of assumptions, alternatives, lock-in risks, exit paths, timing logic, dissent, and revision triggers.
