# Intertemporal Trade-Offs

Intertemporal trade-offs occur when present costs, benefits, risks, and burdens differ from future costs, benefits, risks, and burdens.

Examples:
- efficiency vs redundancy
- delay vs prevention
- commitment vs flexibility
- current welfare vs future welfare
- optimization vs resilience
- short-term savings vs long-term maintenance burden

These trade-offs should be explicit in decision records.
