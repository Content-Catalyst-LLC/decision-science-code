# Modeling Principles

## Resilience is a stock

Resilience capacity can be built, depleted, replenished, or exhausted over time.

## Adaptation requires triggers

Adaptive decision-making requires monitoring indicators, thresholds, fallback options, and revision authority.

## Long horizons change decision structure

Uncertainty compounds, institutions drift, values conflict, feedback loops accumulate, and irreversibility becomes more important over time.

## Short-term efficiency can create long-term fragility

Lean systems can perform well under normal conditions while lacking buffers for shock, stress, or recovery.

## Decision records preserve accountability

Long-horizon decisions require durable records of assumptions, scenarios, trade-offs, thresholds, dissent, and revision rules.
