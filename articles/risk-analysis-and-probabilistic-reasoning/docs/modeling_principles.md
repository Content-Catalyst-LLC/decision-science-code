# Modeling Principles

## Risk combines likelihood and consequence

Risk analysis should connect uncertain events to stakes, exposure, vulnerability, and response capacity.

## Averages are incomplete

Expected loss is useful, but it can conceal volatility, skew, tail exposure, and threshold breach frequency.

## Probability quality matters

Probabilities should be documented by source, evidence quality, confidence, uncertainty range, and stability.

## Stress testing is essential

Risk analysis should test severe plausible scenarios and compound stresses, not only baseline assumptions.

## Risk analysis supports judgment

Models should make assumptions visible and reviewable. They should not replace decision responsibility.
