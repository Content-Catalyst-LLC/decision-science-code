# Modeling Principles

## Scenarios are not forecasts

Scenarios are structured representations of plausible uncertainty. Their value comes from comparing decisions across futures.

## Strategy performance is scenario-dependent

A strategy can look strong in one future and fragile in another.

## Decision rules must be explicit

Expected value, maximin, minimax regret, satisficing, threshold pass rates, and adaptive rules imply different attitudes toward uncertainty.

## Vulnerability matters

Scenario evaluation should identify the conditions under which strategies fail.

## Adaptation must be designed

Monitoring indicators, triggers, fallback options, and revision authority connect scenario evaluation to implementation.

## Decision records preserve accountability

Scenario assumptions, criteria, scores, dissent, trade-offs, and selected rationale should be documented before implementation.
