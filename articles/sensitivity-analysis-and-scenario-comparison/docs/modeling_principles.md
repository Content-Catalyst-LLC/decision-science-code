# Modeling Principles

## Baseline results are not enough

A decision that looks strong under one baseline model may be fragile under plausible variation.

## Sensitivity analysis exposes assumption dependence

Sensitivity analysis tests how outputs, rankings, risk profiles, and action recommendations change when inputs change.

## Scenarios should be coherent

A scenario should represent a plausible combination of conditions, not a random collection of parameter changes.

## Robustness is different from optimization

The best option under one forecast may not be the best option across many plausible futures.

## Decision records should preserve sensitivity findings

Assumption ranges, thresholds, ranking reversals, and review triggers should be documented.
