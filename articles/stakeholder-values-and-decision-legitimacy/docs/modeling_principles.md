# Modeling Principles

## Values are not just preferences

Preferences describe what stakeholders want. Values explain why consequences matter.

## Affectedness is not influence

Powerful groups may have influence without high exposure. Less powerful groups may bear the largest burdens.

## Aggregate benefit can hide concentrated harm

Decision analysis should track maximum burden, threshold failure, and distributional exposure.

## Procedure is part of legitimacy

Voice, transparency, explanation, contestability, and review are not communication extras. They shape whether a decision is defensible.

## Scores are diagnostic

Legitimacy scores help inspect trade-offs. They do not end moral, legal, or political judgment.
