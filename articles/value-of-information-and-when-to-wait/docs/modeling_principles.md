# Modeling Principles

## Information is decision-relative

Information is valuable when it changes action, timing, scale, safeguards, thresholds, or accountability.

## EVPI is an upper bound

Expected value of perfect information estimates the maximum possible value of resolving uncertainty before choosing.

## EVSI evaluates practical evidence

Expected value of sample information evaluates imperfect studies, pilots, tests, forecasts, audits, sensors, and monitoring.

## Delay is not free

Waiting may create opportunity loss, risk exposure, option loss, stakeholder burden, and legitimacy cost.

## Staged action can dominate waiting

When delay is costly but learning matters, decision-makers can act partially, pilot, monitor, or use adaptive triggers.

## Records preserve accountability

Decision records should document uncertainty, evidence-to-action rules, costs, delay risks, stopping rules, and timing rationale.
