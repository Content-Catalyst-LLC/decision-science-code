# Modeling Principles

## Distinguish uncertainty types

Risk, ambiguity, model uncertainty, and deep uncertainty require different analytical treatments.

## Do not invent false precision

A precise probability is not useful if the evidence does not justify it.

## Compare decision criteria

Expected value, expected utility, ambiguity-adjusted value, minimax regret, robustness, and option value answer different decision questions.

## Preserve reversibility

When uncertainty is high and learning is possible, reversible or staged choices may dominate large irreversible commitments.

## Document the reasoning

Decision records preserve assumptions, evidence, scenarios, criteria, rationale, dissent, and review triggers.
